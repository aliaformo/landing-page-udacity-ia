    ChatGPT

    ChatGPT (Chat Generative Pre-Trained Transformer) is a large language model-based chatbot developed by OpenAI, notable for enabling users to refine and steer a conversation towards a desired length, format, style, level of detail, and language used. Successive prompts and replies, are taken into account at each stage of the conversation as a context. Developed by OpenAI and released in 2022. Learn more here

    Main Features

    • It is a sibling model to InstructGPT, which is trained to follow an instruction in a prompt and provide a detailed response.
    • It is fine-tuned from a model in the GPT-3.5 series, which finished training in early 2022.
    • ChatGPT and GPT-3.5 were trained on an Azure AI super computing infrastructure.

    Limitations

    • Sometimes it writes plausible-sounding but incorrect or nonsensical answers.
    • It is sensitive to tweaks to the input phrasing or attempting the same prompt multiple times.
    • The model is often excessively verbose and overuses certain phrases, such as restating that it’s a language model trained by OpenAI.

    Bing AI

    Bing Chat, also known as "the new Bing", is an artificial intelligence (AI) chatbot developed by Microsoft and released in 2023. It is powered by the Microsoft Prometheus model.

    Main features

    • It has been built on top of OpenAI's GPT-4 foundational large language model (LLM), and has been fine-tuned using both supervised and reinforcement learning techniques.
    • It can serve as a chat tool, provide the user with information and insights on the website page open in the browser, and use its Image Creator to design images based on text.
    • Bing Chat can also cite its sources, unlike many other chatbots.

    Limitations

    • It remains available only on Microsoft's Edge browser or Bing app.
    • It produces several hallucinations.
    • Microsoft had to restrict the number of chats and session per day.

    Bard

    Bard is a conversational generative artificial intelligence chatbot developed by Google, based initially on the LaMDA family of large language models (LLMs) and later the PaLM LLM. It was developed as a direct response to the rise of OpenAI's ChatGPT, and was released in a limited capacity in 2023 to lukewarm responses. Learn more here

    Main features

    • Bard is designed as an interface to an LLM that enables users to collaborate with generative AI.
    • It can generate a variety of responses, even from the same or similar prompts and questions.
    • It has a “Google it” button to make it easy for users to check its responses or explore sources on the web.

    Limitations

    • Bard is still an experiment
    • It will not always get it right
    • It may give inaccurate or inappropriate responses.

    Claude 2

    Claude 2 is a powerful model, which excels at a wide range of tasks from sophisticated dialogue and creative content generation to detailed instruction. It is good for complex reasoning, thoughtful dialogue, coding, and detailed content creation among others. Developed by Anthropic and launched in 2023

    Main features

    • It Process mountains of text
    • It has natural conversations
    • It can handle a variety of basic instructions and logical scenarios

    Limitations

    • It may occasionally generate incorrect or misleading information.
    • It may produce offensive or biased content.
    • It doesn't access the internet, so sometimes the responses could be not so updated or integrate all the last-minute information
